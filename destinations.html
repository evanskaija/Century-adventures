TANZANIA WILDERNESS

Wild Landscapes & Pristine Beaches

Embark on a journey through Africa's most breathtaking wild landscapes and pristine islands. From the classic northern safari parks to the untouched southern wilderness and the white-sand coastlines of the Swahili Coast, Tanzania offers unparalleled variety.

Great Migration

Serengeti National Park

The iconic heart of the Great Migration, famous for its endless savannah plains and massive predator populations.

Explore Serengeti
World Heritage

Ngorongoro Crater Area

The world's largest inactive volcanic caldera, forming a natural sanctuary hosting over 25,000 large mammals.

Explore Ngorongoro
Land of Giants

Tarangire National Park

Famous for its majestic ancient baobab trees and harboring Tanzania's largest concentration of elephants.

Explore Tarangire
Flamingo Lake

Lake Manyara National Park

A scenic gem showcasing tree-climbing lions, a massive soda lake with pink flamingos, and groundwater forests.

Explore Manyara
Wild South

Nyerere National Park (Selous)

One of Africa's largest protected areas, offering wild, untamed boat safaris along the mighty Rufiji River.

Explore Selous
Rugged Wilderness

Ruaha National Park

A remote, rugged landscape home to 10% of the world's remaining wild lions, and enormous prides.

Explore Ruaha
Accessible Wild

Mikumi National Park

Often compared to Serengeti for its open savannah horizons, teeming with zebras, wildebeests, and buffaloes.

Explore Mikumi
Untouched Africa

Katavi National Park

Tanzania's most remote park, showing massive pods of hippos and crocodiles battling in shrinking seasonal rivers.

Explore Katavi
Chimp Trekking

Gombe Stream Park

The famous chimpanzee sanctuary located on Lake Tanganyika, where Jane Goodall conducted her pioneering studies.

Explore Gombe
