Tarangire National Park

Exploring the Wildlife Wonders of Tarangire.

OVERVIEW

A Vital Water Source

Located in northern Tanzania, Tarangire National Park is a hidden gem that should not be missed by wildlife enthusiasts.

Covering an area of approximately 2,600 square kilometers, this park is named after the Tarangire River, which provides a vital water source for the diverse range of animals that call this park home.

WILDLIFE

Large Herds of Elephants

Tarangire National Park is home to a wide variety of wildlife, making it a prime destination for game viewing.

Visitors to the park can look forward to sightings of large herds of elephants, along with other impressive creatures such as lions, leopards, cheetahs, and buffalo.

The park is also a birdwatcher’s paradise, with over 550 bird species recorded within its boundaries. During the dry season, the Tarangire River becomes a focal point for wildlife, attracting animals in search of water, offering visitors the opportunity to witness thrilling predator-prey interactions.

Top Things To Do

Bush Walks & Game Drives

Embark on thrilling game drives or explore the park on foot with a guided bush walk for a unique perspective on the flora and fauna of Tarangire.

How to Get There

Located approx 118 kilometers from Arusha, Tarangire is easily accessible by road. The nearest airstrip is Kuro Airstrip for scheduled or charter flights.
